Essential Fantasy

Fantasy is the oldest form of storytelling ‚Äî myths, legends, and tales of impossible things have been with us since before writing. The modern genre stretches from Tolkien's foundational world-building to Joe Abercrombie's brutal deconstruction of its tropes.

For epic fantasy: Begin with Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings if you haven't, then move to Brandon Sanderson's The Way of Kings for modern world-building at its most ambitious.

For grimdark: Joe Abercrombie's The Blade Itself is the genre's defining text. Follow it with Mark Lawrence's Prince of Thorns or John Gwynne's Malice.

For character-driven fantasy: Robin Hobb's Assassin's Apprentice is one of the finest character studies in any genre. Patrick Rothfuss's The Name of the Wind is stunning prose in a fantasy setting.

For short fantasy: Ursula K. Le Guin's The Wind's Twelve Quarters remains the benchmark for short-form fantasy ‚Äî precise, mythic, and quietly devastating.
