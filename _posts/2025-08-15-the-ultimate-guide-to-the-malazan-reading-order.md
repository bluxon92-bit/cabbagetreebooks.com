The Malazan world is vast, complex, and richly imagined—created through the combined efforts of Steven Erikson and Ian C. Esslemont. With several interconnected series spanning centuries, newcomers are often overwhelmed by where to start. This guide breaks down the recommended reading order along with short synopses of each book, ensuring you can fully immerse yourself in the epic narrative.

## **Malazan Book of the Fallen (By Steven Erikson)**

The Malazan Book of the Fallen was the first series released into this fantasy universe. Its 10 volumes, originally published in order, focus on the rise and fall of mighty empires, the interplay of gods and mortals, and the high-stakes personal journeys of unforgettable characters.

1.   **Gardens of the Moon**
  Introduces readers to the sprawling empire and the magical, war-torn world of Malazan. The novel sets the stage with political intrigue, ancient powers, and the early skirmishes that hint at larger conflicts to come.
2.   **Deadhouse Gates**
  This volume follows the dramatic Chain of Dogs—a harrowing military retreat across a hostile land. It explores themes of duty, sacrifice, and the brutal realities of war.
3.   **Memories of Ice**
  In this installment, disparate cultures and factions come together against a common enemy. The merging of military strategy with mysticism and ancient lore creates an unforgettable battle for survival.
4.   **House of Chains**
  Shifting allegiances and sprawling journeys take center stage as characters face increasingly dire destinies. The novel deepens the mythology while exploring the cost of power and ambition.
5.   **Midnight Tides**
  Stepping away from the familiar empire, this book delves into the lives of new peoples and distant lands. It provides insight into the forces driving change in the world of Malazan.
6.   **The Bonehunters**
  The narrative intensifies as legendary battles and heroic figures come to the fore. The characters are pushed to their limits against seemingly insurmountable odds.
7.   **Reaper’s Gale**
  Multiple plotlines converge in this intricate tale, dealing with themes of fate, retribution, and the inexorable march of destiny. It’s a tale where mortal decisions have cosmic ramifications.
8.   **Toll the Hounds**
  This novel carries a mythic tone as divine beings, and mortal champions clash, leading to introspective journeys and morally complex decisions. It emphasizes the heavy price of war and the nature of sacrifice.
9.   **Dust of Dreams**
  As tensions build towards an ultimate confrontation, this book underscores the inevitability of destiny and the human cost of power. The narrative weaves together numerous storylines on the brink of collapse.
10.   **The Crippled God**
  Serving as the explosive conclusion, this final installment reshapes the cosmic order. The fates of gods and mortals intertwine in a dramatic resolution that redefines the world of Malazan.

## **Novels of the Malazan Empire (By Ian C. Esslemont)**

Chronologically aligned with Erikson’s series, the Novels of the Malazan Empire offer a complementary perspective on the empire’s rise, shedding light on events hinted at in the Book of the Fallen.

1.   **Night of Knives**
  This prequel delves into the formation of the empire, revealing early conspiracies and shadowy events that forged the future of Malazan.
2.   **Return of the Crimson Guard**
  Focusing on emerging military heroes and strategic exploits, this installment expands upon the empire’s early military campaigns. Political machinations and warfare blend to create a rich, complex narrative.
3.   **Stonewielder**
  Explores the deep-seated power struggles and legendary battles that serve as the backbone of the empire’s history. The novel builds on the mystical elements that underlie political intrigue.
4.   **Orb Sceptre Throne**
  This volume examines the intricate balance between divine intervention and human ambition. Esslemont offers readers a mix of military strategy interlaced with arcane lore.
5.   **Blood and Bone**
  A brutal chronicle of the empire’s formative conflicts, this book captures the raw power dynamics and the often harsh truths of statecraft and warfare.
6.   **Assail**
  Concluding the series, “Assail” ties together political intrigue, ancient mysteries, and the relentless march of destiny that define the Malazan Empire.

## **Bauchelain & Korbal Broach Novellas (By Steven Erikson)**

These novellas offer a darker, more macabre look at the Malazan world, following the infamous duo Bauchelain and Korbal Broach. Expect a blend of horror, dark humor, and supernatural intrigue.

1.   **Blood Follows**
  The series kicks off with a journey into a bleak and bizarre underworld. Bauchelain and Korbal Broach navigate a realm where horror and wit walk hand-in-hand.
2.   **The Healthy Dead**
  Continuing the unconventional adventures, this novella weaves the supernatural with satirical commentary on life, death, and everything in between.
3.   **The Lees of Laughter’s End**
  It delves into the eerie and humorous side of mortality, bringing forth existential questions through a twisted narrative lens.
4.   **Crack’d Pot Trail**
  A quirky expedition filled with surprises, the story blends absurdity with the darker corners of magic and mortality in the Malazan universe.
5.   **The Wurms of Blearmouth**
  Presents further eerie escapades where the boundaries between the living and the dead blur in unexpected ways.
6.   **The Fiends of Nightmaria**
  Merging elements of horror and humor, this novella continues the duo’s peculiar adventures through supernatural realms.
7.   **Upon a Dark of Evil Overlords**
  Serving as a capstone to the novella series, the narrative wraps up the duo’s arc with a blend of macabre wit and dark fantasy.

## **The Kharkanas Trilogy (By Steven Erikson)**

Set thousands of years before the main events, the Kharkanas Trilogy explores the ancient origins of the Malazan universe, focusing on the birth of gods and primordial power.

1.   **Forge of Darkness**
  This prequel lays down the mythic foundations of the world. It introduces the early struggles for power and the shaping of divine legacies that echo throughout later ages.
2.   **Fall of Light**
  Continuing the journey into antiquity, “Fall of Light” examines the profound consequences of ancient choices, revealing the cost of hubris among gods and mortals alike.
3.   **Walk in Shadow (Upcoming)**
  Anticipated to further illuminate the mysterious forces at play in the ancient past, this forthcoming volume is highly awaited by fans eager for deeper insights into the formation of the Malazan cosmos.

## **The Path to Ascendancy Series (By Ian C. Esslemont)**

This prequel series provides a backdrop to the founding of the Malazan Empire, highlighting the early lives of pivotal characters whose legacies continue to influence the modern narrative.

1.   **Dancer’s Lament**
  Unfolds the beginnings of a character who will later emerge as a cornerstone of the Malazan mythos. It’s a journey marked by personal tragedy and nascent ambition.
2.   **Deadhouse Landing**
  Delivers a closer look at the early days of the empire’s formation, showcasing the blend of fate, ambition, and the harsh realities of carving out a dominion.
3.   **Kellanved’s Reach**
  Chronicles the ascension of one of Malazan’s most influential figures. This volume connects personal narratives with the broader geopolitical shifts that define the empire.

## **The Witness Trilogy (By Steven Erikson)**

Set after the events of *The Crippled God*, the Witness Trilogy explores the long-term consequences of ancient conflicts. It follows characters as they navigate a reshaped world order, balancing the remnants of divine influence with human resilience.

1.   **The God is Not Willing
**Opens the post-epic narrative with a focus on the lingering effects of past battles on a transformed world. Characters face new challenges as they deal with the fallout of divine and mortal conflicts.
2.   **No Life Forsaken
**Continues the exploration of a changed world where old legends still inform new struggles. It’s a narrative of rebuilding, redemption, and the ongoing interplay between gods and men.
3.   **(Untitled Third Novel)
**The forthcoming third installment is expected to wrap up the narrative, tying together threads from previous conflicts while setting a path toward a renewed future in the Malazan realm.
